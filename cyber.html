Cybersecurity

In a world that runs on data, cybersecurity is the shield that protects our digital lives. Learn how threats evolve and how we stay safe online.

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks often aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, extort money, or disrupt normal business operations. Effective cybersecurity requires multiple layers of protection across computers, networks, and programs.

Common Cyber Threats

Malware

Malicious software like viruses, worms, and trojans that damage or gain unauthorized access to systems. It can steal data, encrypt files, or spy on user activity.

Phishing

Fraudulent emails or messages disguised as legitimate sources to trick users into revealing passwords, credit card numbers, or other sensitive information.

Ransomware

A type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands a ransom payment to restore access. Major attacks have targeted hospitals, schools, and governments.

How to Stay Safe Online

  • 1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account
  • 2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • 3. Keep your software and OS updated regularly
  • 4. Never click on suspicious links or attachments
  • 5. Use a reputable antivirus and firewall

Key Areas of Cybersecurity

Network Security

Protects the integrity and usability of network and data using hardware and software.

Cloud Security

Ensures data stored in cloud services is protected from breaches and unauthorized access.

App Security

Focuses on keeping software and devices free from threats during development and deployment.

Data Privacy

Regulates how personal and sensitive information is collected, stored, and shared.
